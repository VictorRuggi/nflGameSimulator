This simulator does not take into account current NFL standings or injuries. Once the simulation is done, you can view a box score of the result at the very bottom of the page.


SCORING BREAKDOWN

Players on offense (QB, RB, WR, TE) uniquely score through touchdowns. Each touchdown is worth 6 points.


Players on special teams (K) score in two different ways: by kicking field goals or by successfully kicking extra point conversions following touchdowns. A field goal is worth 3 points, and as its name implies, extra point conversions are worth 1 point. Under current NFL rules, if a touchdown is scored in overtime, the special teams will not kick as a touchdown automatically ends the game in this case.


Players on defense (DEF) are also capable of scoring in two different ways: by scoring a safety or by scoring a touchdown following a turnover of possession through either recovering the opponent's fumble or intercepting the opponent's pass. Safeties are worth 2 points, and just like with offensive touchdowns, defensive touchdowns are also worth 6 points.
