SCORING BREAKDOWN

Players on offense (QB, RB, WR, TE) uniquely score through touchdowns. Each touchdown is worth 6 points. Touchdown passes do not result in 6 points for the quarterback; only rushing and receiving touchdowns will lead to points being credited to a player's stat line. Quarterbacks can only get credited with points through rushing touchdowns, wide receivers and tight ends can only earn points by catching a touchdown pass, and running backs can earn points both through rushing for a touchdown and by catching a touchdown pass.


Players on special teams (K) score in two different ways: by kicking field goals or by successfully kicking extra point conversions following touchdowns. A field goal is worth 3 points, and as its name implies, extra point conversions are worth 1 point. Under current NFL rules, if a touchdown is scored in overtime, the special teams will not kick as a touchdown automatically ends the game in this case.


Players on defense (DEF) are also capable of scoring in two different ways: by scoring a safety or by scoring a touchdown following a turnover of possession through either recovering the opponent's fumble or intercepting the opposing quarterback's pass. Safeties are worth 2 points, and just like with offensive touchdowns, defensive touchdowns are also worth 6 points.
